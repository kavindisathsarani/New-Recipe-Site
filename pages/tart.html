Fruit Tart Recipe

A Fruit Tart is a delightful dessert featuring a crisp tart shell filled with a creamy custard and topped with fresh, colorful fruits. This recipe combines a buttery tart crust with a rich, vanilla-flavored custard and a variety of fresh fruits, creating a visually stunning and delicious treat. Perfect for special occasions or a sweet treat any time, this fruit tart is sure to impress!

Ingredients:

  • For the Tart Crust:
    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • ¼ teaspoon salt
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
    • 1 large egg yolk
    • 2 tablespoons ice water (or more as needed)
  • For the Custard Filling:
    • 1 cup whole milk
    • ¼ cup granulated sugar
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
    • 1 large egg yolk
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • For the Fruit Topping:
    • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and sliced
    • 1 cup blueberries
    • 1 kiwi, peeled and sliced
    • 1 banana, sliced
    • ¼ cup apricot jam (for glazing, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tart Crust:
    • In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, and salt.
    • Add cold butter pieces and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Stir in the egg yolk and ice water, a tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together.
    • Form the dough into a disc, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface and fit it into a tart pan.
    • Trim the edges and prick the bottom with a fork. Line with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans.
    • Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until golden brown. Let cool completely.
  2. Prepare the Custard Filling:
    • In a medium saucepan, whisk together milk, sugar, and cornstarch.
    • Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook, whisking constantly, until the mixture starts to thicken.
    • In a small bowl, whisk the egg yolk and gradually add a small amount of the hot milk mixture to temper it.
    • Return the egg mixture to the saucepan and continue to cook, whisking constantly, for another 2-3 minutes.
    • Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract. Let the custard cool slightly before pouring it into the cooled tart shell.
  3. Assemble the Tart:
    • Spread the custard evenly in the cooled tart shell.
    • Arrange the fresh fruit on top of the custard in a decorative pattern.
    • If desired, gently warm the apricot jam and brush it over the fruit to give it a glossy finish.
  4. Serve: Chill the tart in the refrigerator until ready to serve. Enjoy your delicious and beautiful fruit tart!
